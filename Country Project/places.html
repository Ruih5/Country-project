Harbin

Harbin is a city in northeastern China known for its amazing ice and snow festivals, Russian architectural style, and rich cultural heritage. Nicknamed "Ice City".

Guangzhou

Guangzhou, also known as Canton, is the capital of Guangdong Province. It is one of the oldest cities in China and the birthplace of Cantonese cuisine.

Hongkong

Hong Kong is a blend of Eastern and Western cultures and is renowned as a global financial center for its iconic skyline, bustling harbor, and rich history.

Nanjing

Nanjing, the capital of Jiangsu Province, is a historic city in eastern China, known for its ancient cultural sites and as one of the four ancient capitals of China.

Beijing

Beijing is the capital of China and the third most populous city in the world. With its rich history, Beijing's history dates back 3,000 years. It is known for its iconic buildings such as the Forbidden City and Tiananmen Square, as well as its cultural significance.

Shanghai

Located on the central coast of China, Shanghai is China's largest city and a global financial center, known for its fusion of modern and traditional Chinese culture.

Xi'an

Xi'an is the capital of Shaanxi Province, China. One of the oldest cities in China, famous for its ancient history, Terracotta Warriors and the starting point of the Silk Road.

Chengdu

CChengdu is the capital of Sichuan Province, with a history dating back to at least the 4th century BC. It is famous for its spicy cuisine, giant pandas, and as a cultural center in southwest China.
